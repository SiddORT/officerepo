Let's improve the architecture and user experience of the Recruitment and Interview Management modules.

IMPORTANT

This is primarily a UI/UX and navigation enhancement.

Do NOT redesign the database.
Do NOT break existing APIs.
Do NOT duplicate interview data.

Interview Management must remain an independent module, but Recruitment should seamlessly integrate with it so users never feel they are switching between unrelated modules.

==================================================
OBJECTIVE
==================================================

The hiring lifecycle should feel continuous.

Job Requisition
↓

Job Opening
↓

Candidate

↓

Interview

↓

Offer

↓

Employee Onboarding

Interview Management remains a standalone module but Candidate becomes the bridge between Recruitment and Interview Management.

==================================================
INTERVIEW INTEGRATION
==================================================

Interview data must always come from Interview Management.

Do NOT duplicate interview records.

Candidate Profile should simply consume interview APIs.

All updates happen inside Interview Management.

==================================================
OFFER INTEGRATION
==================================================

Offer Management remains inside Recruitment.

Candidate Profile simply exposes Offer functionality in context.

200–250ms

==================================================
RESULT
==================================================

The user should never feel they are jumping between Recruitment and Interview Management.

Instead, Candidate Profile should act as the central workspace that connects both modules.

Recruitment owns:

Requisitions

Job Openings

Candidates

Offers

Interview Management owns:

Scheduling

Calendar

Pipelines

Panels

Feedback

Candidate Profile provides a seamless experience by integrating both modules without duplicating functionality.

The final design should feel similar to enterprise ATS platforms like Greenhouse, Lever, BambooHR and Workday while maintaining the existing Office Repo design language.
